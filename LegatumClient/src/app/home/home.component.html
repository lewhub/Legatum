What Are Smart Contracts?

Uploading, securing and stamping your most precious legal-documents to the Ethereum Network.

Contracts, by definition, are legally binding agreements between two or more persons/parties with the intention of exchanging goods and services. Without a doubt, the execution of any agreed upon terms relies solely upon trust. However, trust often fails those even with the best of intentions, leading to fraud, extortion and gruesome legal battles costing thousands of dollars or even worse in the case of bequeathment disputes: family relationships.

Thus, those of us at Legatum seek to remove the fallible element of human trust from the execution of Wills. Ethereum, a blockchain technology platform, employs the use of "Smart Contracts" which upload your encrypted Will of choice into a body of Solidity (Ethereum's programming language) code. Then, your Will and its contents are exported to Ethereum's distributed public-ledger, where any and all changes to every "Smart Contract" ever created are visible for the world to see. Your data however, will be safely "hashed" into a format illegible to humans and can be retrieved for you to view or verify in the case of a passing in your family. We strive for a world without greedy family members appearing with a 'Hidden Will' or forcing the hand of a vulnerable elder.
